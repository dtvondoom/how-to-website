      <div class="container fonts">
        <h2 class="section_title" id="fonts">Fonts</h2>
        <p>Fonts are another way to add a personal touch to your website. For this website I am using the <span class="font_example">Bitter</span> font. Here are some more fonts you can use:</p>
        <div class="font-container">
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label archivo">Archivo</h3>
            <p class="regular archivo">Archivo presents a clean, geometric sans-serif, thoughtfully designed for both digital and print applications. Its technical precision ensures optimal readability in user interfaces and body text, prioritizing efficiency and clarity.</p>
            <p class="bold archivo">Archivo bold amplifies its robust structure, commanding attention for headings and emphasized text, conveying a sense of strength.</p>
            <p class="italic archivo">Archivo italic introduces a subtle slant, adding a touch of dynamism to the font while retaining its technical precision.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label bitter">Bitter</h3>
            <p class="regular bitter">Bitter offers a sturdy slab serif, crafted for comfortable reading on screens across various devices. The slightly condensed letterforms maximize space without sacrificing legibility, resulting in a robust and reliable textual experience.</p>
            <p class="bold bitter">Bitter bold emphasizes its strong serifs, making it excellent for impactful headlines and bold statements, lending a grounded presence.</p>
            <p class="italic bitter">Bitter italic provides a gentle slant, enhancing readability in longer text passages, while maintaining the font's grounded character.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label cabin">Cabin</h3>
            <p class="regular cabin">Cabin showcases a humanist sans-serif, drawing inspiration from classic typefaces while incorporating a modern touch. Its rounded terminals contribute to a friendly and approachable aesthetic, making it highly versatile for diverse design needs.</p>
            <p class="bold cabin">Cabin bold delivers a solid, confident look, making it ideal for headings and sections that require emphasis, giving a strong modern feel.</p>
            <p class="italic cabin">Cabin italic flows with a subtle elegance, adding a touch of sophistication to the text, while keeping the modern humanist style.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label domine">Domine</h3>
            <p class="regular domine">Domine reveals a classic serif typeface, characterized by strong and elegant letterforms. It is meticulously designed for titles and headlines, conveying a timeless and sophisticated feel that commands attention.</p>
            <p class="bold domine">Domine bold strengthens its classic form, making it a commanding choice for impactful headings and titles, displaying a very strong serif look.</p>
            <p class="italic domine">Domine italic introduces a graceful slant, adding a touch of refinement to the text, while retaining its classic serif structure.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label vollkorn">Vollkorn</h3>
            <p class="regular vollkorn">Vollkorn delivers a robust serif typeface, specifically tailored for comfortable reading in extended body text. Its well-balanced proportions and classic design ensure a reliable and pleasant reading experience.</p>
            <p class="bold vollkorn">Vollkorn bold provides a solid presence, making it suitable for emphasizing key phrases and subheadings, and keeping the classic serif look.</p>
            <p class="italic vollkorn">Vollkorn italic offers a smooth, readable slant, enhancing the flow of text, while maintaining its robust serif character.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="font-panel">
            <h3 class="font-label yanone-kaffeesatz">Yanone Kaffeesatz</h3>
            <p class="regular yanone-kaffeesatz">Yanone Kaffeesatz presents a clean, slightly condensed sans-serif, optimized for modern headlines and display uses. Its streamlined design and contemporary aesthetic make it ideal for impactful visual communication.</p>
            <p class="bold yanone-kaffeesatz">Yanone Kaffeesatz bold amplifies its modern, condensed form, making it excellent for bold statements and impactful titles.</p>
            <p class="italic yanone-kaffeesatz">Yanone Kaffeesatz italic introduces a dynamic slant, adding a touch of modern flair to the text, while maintaining its clean aesthetic.</p>

        <h2 class="section_title" id="borders">Borders</h2>
        <p>Finally, you will need borders to wrap your elements in nice little boxes and organize them neatly with divs (divs are a more technical, under-the-hood term, learn more about them <a href="https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Element/div">here</a>). Below you'll see a few border styles you can wrap your content around:</p>
        <div class="border-container">
          <div class="border-panel no-border">
            <h3>No border</h3>
            <p>This completely removes any visible line or outline around an element, effectively making the element's edge invisible.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="border-panel solid-border">
            <h3>Solid border</h3>
            <p>This creates a single, continuous, and unbroken line around the element, providing a clear and defined edge.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="border-panel dotted-border">
            <h3>Dotted border</h3>
            <p>This style displays a series of small, circular dots along the border, producing a visually distinct, segmented line.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="border-panel dashed-border">
            <h3>Dashed border</h3>
            <p>This renders a line composed of short, rectangular dashes, creating a segmented line with a more linear appearance than dotted.</p>
          </div>
          <div class="border-panel double-border">
            <h3>Double border</h3>
            <p>This produces two parallel, solid lines, creating a border with a double-line effect, giving a more substantial look.</p>
